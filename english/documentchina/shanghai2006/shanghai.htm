                                                                                                                                         Summer of 2006 - A Photographer's Shanghai Diary 

 David Yang，Ang 2006

16 July, 2006. The first afternoon in Shanghai I was at the People's Square. All in a sudden a little tattered girl run up to me, “Give me some money, give me some money", she kept begging. Well, beggars are everywhere in Chinese cities, but  this is my first encounter with a English speaking Chinese beggar. Why the girl ask for money in English? maybe because with a big camera on my shoulder, I looked like a western or Japanese tourist.

The girl speaks for one thing; Shanghai is an international metropolis, one of the largest in the world. I don't have much to say about cities. There are skyscrapers and slums, subways and buses, pubs and parks, BMWs and bicycles, gangsters and beggars, restaurants and hotels (no star or 6-star), busy working people and highly skilled thief.............  what else can I say? The basic elements of cities are the same, big cities have more choices and small cities have less, that's the only difference.

Shanghai, a view from the Oriental Pearl Tower. Photo ID: Shanghai805A

 

So why Shanghai? Simple reason, I am building an image library of China, a project called Document China. I need some pictures from Shanghai, otherwise it would be like an image bank of USA without pictures of New York.  BTW,  does the picture above (shanghai805A) look like New York?
